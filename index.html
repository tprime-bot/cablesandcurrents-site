Our Approach

Technology that belongs to the community

This isn't consulting. It's collaboration. You bring the problem and the context. I bring the technical skills. We build something that actually fits.

Start with the community

Every project begins with listening — to board members, residents, volunteers, whoever is closest to the problem. The best solutions come from the people living them, not from a feature list.

Build it together

You're not handed a product — you're part of building it. What gets built first, what gets cut, what gets changed: that's all driven by real feedback from the people who'll use it every day.

The community owns it

No lock-in. No ongoing dependency. Whatever we build together is yours — your data, your code, your tool. I'm here to support it, not to hold it over you.

About

A neighbor with a keyboard and too much free time

I'm Travis Spencer. I live in Santa Barbara, I have a full-time job, and I spend my spare time building technology for the communities around me.

This started from frustration — living in a community where decisions happened in the dark, maintenance requests disappeared, and residents had no voice. I had the skills to fix that, so I did. Now I want to bring that same approach to other communities dealing with the same thing.

This isn't a startup chasing a market. It's a person who genuinely thinks communities deserve better tools, and who has the technical background to build them. I've worked in enterprise software, built hardware from scratch, designed infrastructure, and shipped production web applications. The goal is to put those skills where they can actually help people.

If you're running a community org, an HOA, a neighborhood association, a nonprofit, or anything in between — and you have a tech problem that's slowing you down — let's talk. No pitch deck, no proposal. Just a conversation.
